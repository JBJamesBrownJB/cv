Twenty years of trading-platform habits, in Rust. A Bevy ECS core — Struct-of-Arrays archetypes, a contiguous spatial buffer packed by a zero-allocation parallel counting sort — keeps the hot working set cache-resident. “My L1/2 is my RAM.” It now sustains a million DNA-driven creatures at 90 fps on a single machine — every optimisation measured by a seed-reproducible benchmark harness.

Joined to the richest frontend ecosystem in the world. That Rust core meets JavaScript — PixiJS / WebGL — at a zero-copy seam where most hybrids die: positions cross the boundary as a shared Float32Array over a NAPI-RS ring buffer, interpolated frontend-side with backward reconciliation for smooth motion — no per-frame serialization tax. Two worlds, mechanical sympathy intact.

And built almost entirely by AI agents — an auditable agentic SDLC (named subagents, sprint commands, TDD-enforcing hooks, AGENTS.md contracts), the same methods now shaping the agentic strategy at Schroders.
